CARD CAPTURE — RULES

Goal

Capture every enemy Ace and face card before your deck turns against you. Never let an Ace, Jack, Queen or King reach the Enemy Capture Pile — that's an instant loss.

Round Order

1. Enemy Phase — gaps in the enemy row close up, then new cards are drawn to refill it.
2. Discard Phase — drop any hand cards you don't want (you can't look through your discard pile later).
3. Draw Phase — draw back up to 4 cards.
4. Capture Phase — do exactly one of the following.

Capture

Spend hand cards of one suit, totaling equal or greater than an enemy card's value, to take it into your own discard pile (it'll come back through your deck later). Jack=11, Queen=12, King=13, Ace=14. A Joker copies the suit and value of one other card currently in your hand.

Concede

Let the enemy's Position 1 card win — it and one card of your choice go to the Enemy Capture Pile together.

Sacrifice

Give up two hand cards (never an Ace or face card) to the Enemy Capture Pile in exchange for sending any one enemy row card to the bottom of the enemy deck, delaying it.

Winning

Empty the enemy deck and enemy row completely without ever losing an Ace or face card to the Enemy Capture Pile.

Automatic Stalemate Losses

Two situations end the game early as unwinnable: losing so many cards of one suit to the Enemy Capture Pile that you can never again reach the value of the cheapest uncaptured enemy card in that suit; or holding a hand of nothing but Aces/face cards that can't capture anything currently in the Enemy Row, with nothing eligible to sacrifice.

Card Capture

A solitaire deck builder. Capture every enemy Ace and face card using their own weapons and tools against them — without ever losing one of your own to the Enemy Capture Pile.
