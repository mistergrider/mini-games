The Bogey

Play every card in the deck into as few columns as you can. Each column runs one suit, strictly downward in rank (K → A, gaps allowed). At the end of your turn, the Bogey flips a card from the deck — you must place it immediately, wherever it legally fits. Choose badly for too long and you'll run out of room.

How to Play

  • Each turn you may play any number of cards from your hand onto the table, and/or discard cards you don't want to a discard pile.
  • A card can start a brand-new column, or be added below the current bottom card of an existing column if it's the same suit and a lower rank. Gaps in rank are fine.
  • There is a hard limit of 12 columns on the table.
  • When you end your turn, the Bogey flips the top card of the draw pile and you must place it immediately — legally, wherever you choose. If it fits nowhere, the game is lost.
  • Your hand refills to full afterward. When the draw pile runs dry, the discard pile is shuffled to replace it.
  • You win once every card is on the table — the fewer columns you used, the better. Beat your chosen target to claim victory.
